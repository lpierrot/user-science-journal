Blogging About Design

Excited to share my thoughts and findings

Posted by Lionel Pierrot on May 7, 2019

Purpose and Scope

For this week's analysis, I conducted a usability test for Swahili Village website. A few years ago, when visiting a couple of friends in Maryland, and they recommended we go to eat at Swahili Village. The food is very delicious but also a bustling restaurant, and we had to wait for some time before we could get seated. I wished I had booked a reservation before going there.

This week when reading Steve Krug's book on usability, this restaurant's website was the first to come to mind. To conduct the test, I recruited three participants and were each given a specific task to complete, and I analyzed the user-performance, user-satisfaction on a task, looked at how participants navigate the website, functionalities, website content and identified design flows.

Participants

I recruited three participants; age range was from 20 years to 35 years.

  • Tester 1: Male Software Engineer with bachelor’s degree.
  • Tester 2: Male Customer Service Agent with some college level.
  • Tester 3: Female Program Manager with a bachelor’s degree.

Schedule & Location

I contacted three participants via text message for a one on one meeting at LLMLC , between 9AM and 12PM. The first participant started schedule at 9AM, second participant 10AM and the last participant at 12PM. Each test lasted about 30 minutes, with 15 minutes explaining them what the test is going to cover and the other 15 minutes were allocated to the test.

Equipment

Equipment used wer a MacBook Pro 13 in, windows desktop and iPhone 8 Plus to record and collect data. Three different browsers (Google Chrome, Firefox, Safari) were also used thoughtout this test.

Scenarios

For testing scenarios, I asked the participants to complete four tasks and a few follow up questions:

  • Make an online reservation: Can you make an online reservation?
  • Find Menu: Can you identify what you would like to order?
  • Find the time for happy hour menu and specials: Can you tell me what time is happy hour? and specials?
  • Cuisine influence/background: Can you identify the background of this cuisine?

Feedback

After finishing each test, I asked the participants to rate their top three usability problems

Tester 1

  • Confusion on the drop-down menu
  • Confusing reservation page
  • List Happy hour page to the menu page
Tester 2
  • Categorize very well the drop-down menu
  • Make it clear how to make reservations
  • Make the happy hour info clear preferably on the menu page
Tester 3
  • Doesn’t understand the about page
  • Doesn’t understand the drop-down option on the menu page
  • Did not like the video on the homepage thought it was loud and outdated
